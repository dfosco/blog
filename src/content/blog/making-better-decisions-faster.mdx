It’s June 2025, and it feels like everywhere you turn there’s someone saying **you should use AI to become more productive**, or trying to telling you how to do so. 

If this makes you feel anxious and exhausted, you’re not alone. Hell, I’m a designer working on AI products, and I’m exhausted too.

I think the worst of it all is the *vagueness* of the advice being thrown around. AI can mean [many different things at once](https://bsky.app/profile/veni.dev/post/3lrzusl33ms2x), and I don’t see basic follow up questions being raised:

- When can AI be actually useful, and when does it get in the way?
- How can it be used meaningfully in a UX design process?
- How to work around its inherent flaws and make the most of it?
- What does leadership actually mean when they say “I want you to use AI”?

---

My take: AI is only useful when it’s helping us **make better decisions, faster**. Anything else is noise.

**filesystem.cc** is a public log of my workflows to make better decisions. The apps, AI models, and strategies that actually work for me — and when they don’t, how I build custom tools on the fly to help me bridge the gaps.
